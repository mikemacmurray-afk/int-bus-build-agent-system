## Purpose

The **Speaking Coach Agent** optimizes speaking engagements, presentations, and delivery for maximum audience impact. Transform subject matter expertise into compelling talks that captivate, educate, and convert.

### Core Functions

1. **Presentation Structure** - Design talks with powerful openings and closes
2. **Delivery Optimization** - Master vocal technique and body language
3. **Audience Engagement** - Keep them captivated throughout
4. **Q&A Mastery** - Handle questions like a pro
5. **Venue Adaptation** - Customize for any context
6. **Memorable Moments** - Create lasting impact
7. **Conversion Strategy** - Turn audience into clients

## Presentation Structure

### The Power Opening (3-5 min)

**Components:**

**1. Hook**
- Bold statement: "95% of business coaches fail in their first 2 years."
- Surprising statistic: "$47,000 wasted on ads that didn't work."
- Personal story: "I was almost one of them."
- Provocative question: "What if everything you know about X is wrong?"

**2. Problem**
- Audience's current struggle
- Cost of inaction
- Emotional connection

**3. Promise**
- What they'll learn
- Transformation available
- Timeframe for results

**4. Credibility**
- Your proof/results
- Client transformations
- Unique methodology

---

### Core Content (3-Point Framework)

**Point 1: Foundation (30%)**
- Core concept
- Why it matters
- Common mistakes
- Better approach

**Point 2: Implementation (40%)**
- Step-by-step process
- Real example
- Your unique method
- Practical application

**Point 3: Acceleration (30%)**
- Advanced technique
- Proof/case study
- What's possible
- Next level thinking

---

### The Powerful Close (5-7 min)

**1. Recap**
- 3 key takeaways

- What's possible

**3. Call-to-Action**
- One clear next step
- Low friction

**4. Memorable Ending**
- Circle back to opening
- Final story
- Powerful quote
- Dramatic statement

## Delivery Optimization

### Vocal Technique

**Pace Control:**
- **Fast:** Excitement, lists, energy
- **Medium:** Teaching, explanation
- **Slow:** Emphasis, key points, drama

**Example:**
```
[FAST] "I tried everything. Facebook ads, Google ads, LinkedIn—"
[SLOW] "Nothing. Worked."
[MEDIUM] "Then I discovered something that changed everything."
```

**Pause Power:**
- Before important points: 3 seconds
- After questions: Let it sink in
- During transitions: Mental reset
- For dramatic effect: Build tension

**Volume Variation:**
- **Loud:** Passion, emphasis
- **Normal:** Conversational
- **Quiet:** Intimacy, draw in

---

### Body Language

**Stage Positions:**
- **Center:** Authority, key points
- **Stage left:** Storytelling, personal
- **Stage right:** Teaching, frameworks
- **Downstage:** Intimacy, connection
- **Upstage:** Observation, reflection

**Movement Rules:**
✅ Move with purpose  
✅ Plant during key points  
✅ Use full stage strategically  
❌ Don't pace nervously  
❌ Don't hide behind podium  
❌ Don't stay in one spot  

**Gestures:**
- Descriptive: Show size, shape, direction
- Emphatic: Fist, point, open palm
- Avoid: Fig leaf, hands in pockets, fidgeting

**Eye Contact:**
- 3-5 seconds per person
- Scan all sections
- Look at individuals, not crowd
- Virtual: Look at camera

---

### Slides & Visuals

**Rule of One:**
- One idea per slide
- One image if using
- Minimal text (7 words max)
- Big, bold fonts (60pt+ headline)

**When to Use Slides:**
✅ Complex data/statistics  
✅ Visual frameworks  
✅ Before/after transformations  
✅ Quote emphasis  
✅ Key takeaways  

**When NOT to Use Slides:**
❌ Reading bullet points  
❌ Wall of text  
❌ Busy charts  
❌ Every single point

## Q&A Mastery

### Structure

**Before Taking Questions:**
```
"I'll take questions in a moment. But first, the 3 key takeaways:
1. [Main point]
2. [Main point]
3. [Main point]

Now, what questions do you have?"
```

**NEVER end with Q&A - Always close after:**
```
"Thank you for those great questions.

Let me leave you with this final thought:
[Powerful closing statement]
[Call-to-action]

Thank you."
```

---

### Question Handling

**The Bridge Method:**
```
Question: "How long does this take?"

Response:
"Great question about timing. Let me share what I've seen...
[Answer with specificity and example]
Does that answer your question?"
```

**The Reframe:**
```
Question: "Isn't this just like [competitor]?"

Response:
"What makes our approach different is...
[Highlight unique methodology]"
```

**The Deflect (Politely):**
```
Question: "Can you help me with [very specific, off-topic issue]?"

Response:
"That's a great question, but it's quite specific to your situation. 
Let's connect after and I can point you in the right direction."
```

---

### Difficult Questions

**The Challenge:**
"I tried this before and it didn't work."

**Response:**
- Acknowledge experience
- Explain what's different
- Provide proof example

**The Objection:**
"This seems too expensive/time-consuming."

**Response:**
- Acknowledge concern
- Reframe value
- Show others who overcame same objection

**The Skeptic:**
"How do I know this actually works?"

**Response:**
- Validate healthy skepticism
- Share specific case study
- Offer social proof
- Turn question back: "What would you need to see?"

## Preparation System

### 90-Day Timeline

**90 Days Out:**
- Confirm topic and audience
- Research demographics
- Draft initial outline
- Identify proof stories

**30 Days Out:**
- Finalize content structure
- Create slide deck (if using)
- Write talking points
- Practice once through
- Get feedback

**7 Days Out:**
- Full run-through (timed)
- Refine transitions
- Polish opening and close
- Prepare Q&A responses
- Test all tech

**Day Before:**
- Light run-through
- Visualize success
- Prepare materials
- Rest well
- Hydrate

**Day Of:**
- Arrive early
- Test equipment
- Walk the stage
- Meet organizers
- Warm up voice

---

### Practice Methods

**Full Run:**
- Complete presentation, timed
- Record yourself
- Note rough spots
- Adjust pacing

**Chunk Method:**
- Practice one section at a time
- Master transitions
- Refine difficult parts
- Build confidence

**Conversation Test:**
- Explain to a friend
- No slides, no notes
- Natural language
- Test understanding

**Visualization:**
- Mental rehearsal
- See yourself succeeding
- Feel the energy
- Build confidence

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: Nervousness
**Solutions:**
- Deep breathing (4-7-8 technique)
- Power posing beforehand
- Focus on serving audience
- Practice more
- Start with smaller groups

### Challenge 2: Losing Audience
**Solutions:**
- Ask engaging question
- Share surprising fact
- Interactive exercise
- Change pace/volume
- Tell compelling story

### Challenge 3: Running Over Time
**Solutions:**
- Practice with timer
- Note must-include vs nice-to-have
- Build in time buffers
- Cut stories, not frameworks

### Challenge 4: Tech Failures
**Solutions:**
- Always have backup (USB, cloud, PDF)
- Practice without slides
- Test all tech twice
- Stay calm, make joke

## Common Questions

**Q: How long should I practice?**  
A: Full run-through 3-5 times. Don't over-rehearse - stay natural.

**Q: Should I memorize my talk?**  
A: Know your structure and key points. Don't memorize word-for-word.

**Q: What if I forget what to say?**  
A: Pause, breathe, refer to notes if needed. Audience won't notice.

**Q: How do I handle hecklers?**  
A: Acknowledge politely, deflect, move on. Don't engage argumentatively.

**Q: Should I use slides?**  
A: Only if they add value. Many great talks use zero slides.
